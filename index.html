"It is 10:00 am and we are opening." that's how I start my cashier shift on a Saturday. I am currently working at the UCR dining hall(Lothian). I work there as a cashier, server, restocking, cleaning, and dishwashing. My workplace is a little different where we, student workers get to do every type of work. Our work section rotates in each shift. Working in the dining hall made me more aware of time management and it helped me to manage my time wisely, as I have to balance work, study, and life. It also pushed me to get out of my comfort zone and made me more spontaneous in talking to strangers. Due to my professional experience, I consider myself an expert at making small talk.

When I started this job I aimed to build more of my leadership skills and go out of my comfort zone. Working as a cashier gave me some level of confidence in my leadership skills. As a cashier, we see different types of people and face different kinds of situations every day. Working with these differences on a daily basis eventually made me capable of handling them and being ready for anything. Nonetheless, I'm still striving to achieve it as we continue to grow every day.

The importance of showing integrity in my workplace is greater since it is a food establishment where people trust that they will receive good and healthy food. We believe and depend on each other to fulfill our part with devotion and honesty. It was through this profession that I learned the importance of working together in a team.
